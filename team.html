AILEEN LEE

Founder

Aileen has many years of experience helping start ups build products customers love, and helping companies scale, both as an investor and operator.

Prior to Cowboy, she joined the venture firm Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers in 1999. At KPCB she worked hands-on with a range of consumer, enterprise, media and greentech companies including Bloom Energy, Blue Nile (NILE), Good Technology (acquired by MOT), One Kings Lane, Plum District, Rent the Runway, Shopkick, Tellme (acquired by MSFT), and Trendyol. During that time she was also founding CEO of RMG Networks (RMGN), the leading digital out-of-home media company, backed by KPCB. Aileen has also held operating roles at Gap Inc., The North Face and Odwalla and started her career at Morgan Stanley.

She has degrees from MIT, Harvard Business School, and Millburn High School; and she's the proud mom of 3 kids plus a dog, chickens and bees, and the wife of a series A stage co-founder/CTO.

NOAH LICHTENSTEIN

Partner

Noah has been helping build start ups in Silicon Valley for nearly a decade, and is passionate about turning ideas into products and businesses that customers love.

Prior to joining Cowboy, Noah was a founding member of HomeRun, a leading social commerce platform that reached over 100 million consumers at its peak (acquired by Rearden Commerce). Noah built and managed the sales infrastructure, led strategic partnerships with AT&T, Google, Facebook, and Amazon, and helped develop the company's B2B commerce platform. Before HomeRun, Noah led consumer business development at the Climate Corporation (acquired by Monsanto (MON)), was the Co-Founder & President of Annuvia, and served as a wildland firefighter in rural Oregon.

He attended Stanford University and serves on the Board of Directors for the United Way of the Bay Area.

ANDY JOHNS

Growth Ninja

Andy is an amazing resource on all things related to customer growth. He spent the last 7 years as a product manager and marketer on user growth and engagement at three hyper-growth companies: Facebook, Twitter and Quora. He helps us consider potential investments, and works with portfolio companies to share insights and build growth strategies.

ERIC FENG

Technology Ninja

Eric is an awesome technologist and product leader who helps us evaluate potential investments and is a sounding board and advisor to portfolio companies. He’s currently the CTO at Flipboard, a personal magazine used by more than 80MM people. Previously he was the founder/CEO of Erly, a social startup funded by KPCB (where Eric was also a Partner). He was also founding CTO and 2nd person at Hulu where he built and led the technology and product organizations and personally coded large parts of Hulu.com; and he worked at Micrsoft and Trilogy Software.

CHRIS POULIOT

Data Science Ninja

Chris Pouliot is a real life rocket scientist, who has also spun astronauts until they were motion sick, split atoms to make an aircraft carrier go fast, provided insightful analysis that led to Google to change their top ad color, and helped Netflix determine what movies and TV shows to buy, and how much they should pay. In his free time, he helps advise many Cowboy portfolio companies on analytics and data infrastructure.

TINA SHARKEY

Marketing / Branding / Coaching Ninja

Tina Sharkey is an amazing brand builder, marketer and storyteller, and she loves coaching early stage companies and coaching founders. She has created and scaled a number of consumer and media businesses as the CEO of Babycenter; co-founder of iVillage; leader of multiple businesses at AOL and the founder of digital for Sesame Workshop. She serves on the boards of HomeAway and Brit+Co and is also a guest lecturer at the Stanford GSB.

LAUREN KOLODNY

MBA All-Star

Lauren is a second year MBA student at the Stanford Graduate School of Business where she spends her time focused on entrepreneurship and venture capital. She has been working with Cowboy Ventures on investments, portfolio advisory and marketing initiatives since January.

Prior to business school, Lauren worked in product marketing at Google where she led a number of launches for the Google Apps for Business team including Google Drive. Lauren also worked in product management for a solar startup and for the Clinton Foundation in India on large scale clean technology projects.

DANNY TRINH

Design Ninja

Danny has designed products at Path and Digg over the last 5 years. He believes good design is as little design as possible. He has been featured in Forbes’ 30 Under 30, Fast Company’s 100 Most Creative People, and is his mother’s favorite son. Today he is building his first company.
