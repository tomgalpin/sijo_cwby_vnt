AILEEN LEE

Founder

Cowboy Ventures was founded by Aileen Lee in 2012. Prior to Cowboy, Aileen had been a partner with the venture firm Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers (KPCB) since 1999.

She has been lead or supporting partner in backing a broad variety of companies including Bloom Energy, Blue Nile, Callaway Digital Arts, Good Technology, One Kings Lane, Plum District, Rent the Runway, Shopkick, Tellme, and Trendyol. She was also founding CEO of RMG Networks, the leading digital out-of-home media company, backed by KPCB.

Prior to joining KPCB, Aileen held roles at Gap Inc., The North Face, Odwalla, and Morgan Stanley. She has degrees from MIT, Harvard Business School, and Millburn High School.

NOAH LICHTENSTEIN

Founder

Noah Lichtenstein is a Partner at Cowboy Ventures.
Prior to joining Cowboy, Noah was a founding member of HomeRun, a leading social commerce platform that reached over 100 million consumers at its peak (acquired in 2011 by Rearden Commerce). In that role, Noah built and managed the company's original sales team, led strategic partnerships with AT&T, Google, Facebook, and Amazon, and helped develop the company's B2B commerce platform, OfferEngine.

Before HomeRun, Noah led consumer Business Development efforts at WeatherBill (n/k/a the Climate Corporation) and was the Co-Founder & President of Annuvia. He attended Stanford University and currently serves on the Board of Directors for the United Way of the Bay Area.

ANDY JOHNS

Ninja

Andy has spent the last 7 years helping consumer startups figure out how to grow, having previously been a product manager and internet marketer working on user growth and engagement for Facebook, Twitter and Quora. Today I'm an EIR at Greylock and working on figuring out what I want to do next.

I like working with Cowboy ventures because the team is fun and sharp, decision-making happens quickly when it comes to figuring out which companies we want to invest in and help grow, and I'm able to have a meaningful impact on our portfolio companies by sharing my knowledge with them.

ERIC FENG

Ninja

Eric Feng is currently the Chief Technology Officer at Flipboard, a personal magazine used by more than 80MM people. Previously Eric was the founder and CEO of Erly, a social startup funded by Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers (where Eric was also a Partner) and acquired in 2012. Before joining Kleiner Perkins, Eric was the founding CTO at Hulu, a joint venture of NBC Universal, News Corp. and The Walt Disney Company.
